CHOCOLATE BRIOCHE


Brioche is a pastry of French origin that is similar to a highly enriched bread, and whose high egg and butter content give it a rich and tender crumb. It is "light and slightly puffy, more or less fine, according to the proportion of butter and eggs. It has a dark, golden, and flaky crust, frequently accentuated by an egg wash applied after proofing. Brioche is made in the same basic way as bread, but has the richer aspect of a pastry because of the extra addition of eggs, butter, liquid (milk, water, cream, and, sometimes, brandy) and occasionally a bit of sugar.
Mix flour, sugar,eggs and yeast and mix for 2 or 3 minutes at medium speed.
Add the salt and mix for about 10 minutes until the dough start to be together and separates from the bowl.
Add the soft butter in pieces and one by one while you mixing and until all the butter is properly incorporated.
Once the butter is in, add the chocolate and mix gently.
Once the chocolate is in, cover with a kitchen cloth or cling film and rest for a couple of hours in a warn place for the proofing. or in the oven at 30 degrees if you are in a hurry .... like me. I'm always running late ...
If you use the oven option the chocolate will melt and will incorporated to the mix, instead of been like chocolate chips brioche will be a chocolate brioche.
Knead the dough and divide in 5 or 6 balls, Knead the again and put them into a 22cm non stick cake tin or just brush with butter and dust with flour if a non stick tin isn't around.
Let the balls to proof until they double the volume. Add some moscovado sugar on top.
LOnce it's cooked, rest to room temperature.
Dust with icing sugar ....
and go ahead!!
